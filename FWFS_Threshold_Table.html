How the scores combine

Weighted blend. Each outlook window (0–6 hr, 6–24 hr, Day 2, Day 3) blends its relevant product scores using fixed weights, so no single soft signal can flip a quiet day on its own. Short-range windows lean on radar, short-fuse QPF, and atmospheric moisture; Day 2 and Day 3 lean on the ERO, SPC, and the WPC and NDFD daily rainfall forecasts.

Watches and warnings set a floor. An active Flood Watch forces the affected window to at least HIGH, and an active Flood or Flash Flood Warning forces at least CRITICAL, regardless of how quiet the other products are. Watches apply only to the windows their valid times actually cover — a watch for today does not raise the Day 2 or Day 3 outlook.

AFD language informs, but does not override. Plain-language signals from the Grand Junction forecast discussion (scores 1–2) feed the blend and the NWS Watch/Warn + AFD indicator, but only actual watch and warning products can force the overall level upward.

Day 3 note. NDFD rainfall grids extend about 72 hours, so the Day 3 daily total can exclude the final early-morning hours of that day. Coverage is tracked and displayed with the data.
