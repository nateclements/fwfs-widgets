How this works

Live data. Pre-modeled flooding.
This map does not model flooding in real time. It selects from a library of flood scenarios that were modeled in advance, using live rainfall data to pick the closest match.
1

Building the representative storm

We analyzed the rainfall patterns of roughly ten real monsoon storms over the Mill Creek and Castle Creek watersheds using gauge-adjusted radar rainfall, and combined them into a single representative storm for this area.

2

Building the scenario ensemble

We placed that storm at four locations where monsoon storm centers typically develop (three in the Mill Creek watershed, one in Castle Valley) and scaled its rainfall from 50% up to 150% of the representative storm. Each storm location and rainfall level combination was run through a detailed hydraulic model (HEC-RAS) ahead of time to map where floodwater would go and how deep it would get.

The percentages scale how much total rain falls, not how the storm unfolds over time. A 150% scenario is the same storm dropping one and a half times as much rain; a 50% scenario is the same storm at half. The timing pattern stays the same across every level.

3

Matching live rainfall

When rain falls over the watersheds, this system automatically compares live radar rainfall estimates (NOAA MRMS) against the ensemble and displays the pre-modeled scenario that most closely matches the storm's location and intensity, along with the buildings inside that scenario's flood extent.

Real storms rarely match a scenario exactly. A storm centered between two modeled locations, or stronger than the largest modeled scenario, can produce flooding that differs from what is shown. Treat this display as guidance, not a precise prediction.
