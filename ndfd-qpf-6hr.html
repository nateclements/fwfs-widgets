NDFD QPF (6 Hour)

National Digital Forecast Database — Quantitative Precipitation Forecast

What you're looking at

This map shows the National Weather Service's official gridded precipitation forecast for an upcoming 6-hour window. Unlike a single forecast office's product, the NDFD is stitched seamlessly across office boundaries, so the values flow continuously across the whole area.

Read it as: “Here's how much rain the NWS officially expects to fall over the next 6 hours.”

What it does

  • Shows the authoritative NWS forecast of where and how much precipitation is expected in the short term.
  • Helps you stage crews, pre-position resources, and anticipate which basins may be at risk before rain begins.
  • Gives a consistent, seamless picture across the full operational area.

What it does NOT do

  • It is not live radar and not a measurement of rain that has already fallen — for that, use the MRMS QPE products.
  • It's a forecast, so actual totals and timing can shift as the event evolves.
  • It does not assess flash-flood severity on its own — pair it with Flash Flood Guidance and observed QPE for the full picture.

Operational note: This display is provided for situational awareness only and supplements — but does not replace — official National Weather Service forecasts, watches, and warnings. Forecast precipitation is inherently uncertain; decisions affecting life and property should always be confirmed against the latest official NWS guidance and local observations.
